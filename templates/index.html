Rectangle

A rectangle has four sides and four right angles.
Triangle
Area formula: A = ½ × b × h
Volume formula: N/A
Where b is base and h is height.
A triangle has three sides and the sum of its angles is 180°.
Pyramid
Area formula: A = base area + ½ × perimeter × slant height
Volume formula: V = (1/3) × base area × height
Where base area is the area of the base, height is the vertical height.
A pyramid is a polyhedron formed by connecting a polygonal base and a point called the apex.
Sphere
Area formula: A = 4 × π × r²
Volume formula: V = (4/3) × π × r³
Where r is the radius.
A sphere is a perfectly round geometrical object in three-dimensional space.
Cylinder
Area formula: A = 2 × π × r × (r + h)
Volume formula: V = π × r² × h
Where r is the radius and h is the height.
A cylinder has two parallel circular bases connected by a curved surface.
Trapezium
Area formula: A = ½ × (a + b) × h
Volume formula: N/A
Where a and b are the lengths of the parallel sides and h is the height.
A trapezium has one pair of parallel sides.
Parallelogram
Area formula: A = b × h
Volume formula: N/A
Where b is the base and h is the height.
A parallelogram has opposite sides that are equal and parallel.
Pentagon
Area formula: A = 1/4 × √(5(5 + 2√5)) × s²
Volume formula: N/A
Where s is the length of a side.
A pentagon has five sides and five angles.
Hexagon
Area formula: A = (3√3/2) × s²
Volume formula: N/A
Where s is the length of a side.
A hexagon has six sides and six angles.
Cuboid
Area formula: A = 2(lb + lh + wh)
Volume formula: V = l × w × h
Where l is length, w is width, and h is height.
A cuboid has six rectangular faces.
Cone
Area formula: A = πr(r + l)
Volume formula: V = (1/3)πr²h
Where r is the radius and l is the slant height.
A cone has a circular base and a single vertex.
Rhombus
Area formula: A = (d1 × d2) / 2
Volume formula: N/A
Where d1 and d2 are the lengths of the diagonals.
A rhombus is a quadrilateral with all sides equal in length.
Ellipse
Area formula: A = π × a × b
Volume formula: N/A
Where a is the semi-major axis and b is the semi-minor axis.
An ellipse is a shape that resembles a stretched circle.
Sector
Area formula: A = (θ/360) × πr²
Volume formula: N/A
Where r is the radius and θ is the central angle in degrees.
A sector is a portion of a circle enclosed by two radii and an arc.
